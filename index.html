The Boys' Club of New York

The Boys’ Club of New York needed a new website to help support their mission. We began with several stakeholder interviews to understand their landscape of online community involvement. After establishing our key users we interviewed several parents of members and many donors to discover what they were missing.

We created a mobile first site that provides direct value to parents seeking reliable, daily scheduling information, as well as a compelling and convenient portal for donations.

FlatRate Moving

My team at Ironpaper was hired to overhaul FlatRate's website--helping the company better communicate their value proposition, attract new customers, and drive sales. We focused on solidifying FlatRate's web presence and building an inbound program to support longterm growth.

We designed a strategic web presence for mobile and refined the desktop experience to engage key personas and improve overall conversion rates for FlatRate Moving.

Hexagon Manufacturing Intelligence

Hexagon MI, (a leading global provider of information technologies that drive productivity and quality across geospatial and industrial enterprise applications) came to us seeking to stand out in the increasingly crowded portable coordinate measuring machine (PCMM) market. Their primary product in this field, the ROMER Absolute Arm, is extremely versatile and has applications well beyond the industrial sector.

Using SEO and content strategy my team created new user pathways to expand marketing reach of the ROMER arm with a new microsite.

Canvs

Canvs is a sentiment analysis platform focused on quantifying audience reactions to specific television airings. They scan social media to discover reactions, then apply emotional tags to offer depth and dimenson. Their tools help TV executives and marketing analysts gauge the effect of their work, as well as target opportunities for future campaigns.

As part of my classwork at General Assembly, Canvs reached out to us with the goal of streamlining their user interface. By the time our project was due, they had already gone live with some of the insights we discovered through user testing and prototyping.
